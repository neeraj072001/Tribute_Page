Biography

Steve Jobs, the American businessman and technology visionary who is best known as the co-founder, chairman, and chief executive officer of Apple Inc, was born on February 24, 1955. His parents were two University of Wisconsin graduate students, Joanne Carole Schieble and Syrian-born Abdulfattah Jandali. They were both unmarried at the time. Jandali, who was teaching in Wisconsin when Steve was born, said he had no choice but to put the baby up for adoption because his girlfriend's family objected to their relationship.

Achievements

Apple (1976) -
With Steve Wozniak, Jobs founded Apple Inc. in 1976 and transformed the company into a world leader in telecommunications
Apple II (1977) -
In 1977 the Apple II was launched and became one of the most popular personal computers. Going into the 80’s and 90’s it became even more popular.
Lisa (1983) -
This computer was named after Jobs’ first daughter and came out in 1983. It was the base of all graphical computer interfaces.
Pixar (1986) -
The Pixar we know today was not the same company Steve Jobs invested $5 million in. He brought in computer graphics to the company. After, he signed a contract Pixar and started making animated films. Since then, Pixar has earned 26 Academy Awards, 7 Golden Globes, and 3 Grammy Awards!
iMac (1998) -
After returning to Apple in 1997. In 1998 the iMac came out and Apple became even more famous. It had the monitor and computer enclosed in plastic. It was a huge success and became a high demand.
iPod (2001) -
This portable digital music player came out in 2001. The iPod lead Apple into the world of music. Since October 2004, the iPod line has had over 90 percent of the market share for digital music player sales in the U.S. Now, they also have the iPod nano and the iPod touch and a 2 nd edition iPod touch.
iTunes Music Store (2003) -
This straight forward solution to buying songs changed the music industry forever. In 2003 it came out and in the first week they had sold a total of 1 million songs. Now, iTunes is used so much and is so popular there is an average buy of 5 billion songs and 50,000 movies a day. iTunes is now the #1 music retailer in the U.S.
iPhone (2007) -
The iPhone came out in 2007 and was called “the invention of 2007,” in Time magazine. Apple is now the world’s most profitable maker of phones. Just recently Apple came out with its 5 th edition of the iPhone the day before Steve Jobs died.
Pad (2010) -
The iPad came out in 2010. This was a touch screen and about the size as a piece of paper. It became an instant success. The iPad sold 15 million units in nine months! The iPad now has a 2 nd edition. This one also includes a camera and video option.
